# Hero Sections Skill

# Purpose

You are responsible for designing premium hero sections for a luxury gold and jewelry brand.

The hero section is the first impression of the company.

Within the first 5 seconds users must understand:

• Who we are
• What we sell
• Why we are different
• What they should do next

The hero must create emotion before asking for action.

---

# Hero Philosophy

Luxury hero sections are calm.

They never shout.

They never feel crowded.

They tell a story.

The hero should immediately communicate quality, trust and exclusivity.

---

# Layout

A hero should contain only the following:

Headline

↓

Supporting Text

↓

Primary CTA

↓

Optional Secondary CTA

↓

Premium Visual

↓

Trust Indicators

Nothing else.

---

# Headline Rules

Headline must be short.

5–12 words.

Strong.

Elegant.

Easy to scan.

Never write paragraphs as headlines.

Examples

Timeless Gold Jewelry

Crafted For Every Generation

Luxury Designed To Last

Investment Meets Beauty

Avoid clickbait headlines.

---

# Supporting Text

One short paragraph.

Explain value.

Do not explain every feature.

Focus on emotion and trust.

Maximum 2–3 lines.

---

# CTA Rules

Only one primary CTA.

Examples

Shop Collection

Explore Jewelry

View Products

Start Shopping

Optional secondary CTA

Learn More

Our Story

View Collection

Never place multiple competing actions.

---

# Hero Image

Hero image is the centerpiece.

Requirements

Professional photography

Real jewelry

Luxury lighting

Neutral background

Large composition

High resolution

Never use stock-looking images.

---

# Composition

The eye should move naturally:

Headline

↓

CTA

↓

Jewelry

↓

Trust Section

Never force users to search.

---

# White Space

Large breathing room.

Luxury requires empty space.

Do not fill every area.

---

# Typography

Large headline.

Elegant.

Readable.

Comfortable spacing.

Premium hierarchy.

No decorative fonts.

---

# Colors

Mostly neutral.

Gold used only as an accent.

Avoid colorful backgrounds.

Avoid busy gradients.

Luxury comes from simplicity.

---

# Trust Elements

Below the hero include small trust items.

Examples

Certified Gold

Secure Payment

Nationwide Delivery

Authenticity Guarantee

Premium Packaging

Excellent Customer Support

Display with minimal icons.

---

# Background

Allowed

White

Warm White

Soft Gray

Dark Charcoal

Muted Texture

Forbidden

Busy images

Loud gradients

Pattern-heavy backgrounds

---

# Hero Height

Hero should feel spacious.

Avoid full-screen heroes unless they genuinely improve the experience.

Users should see the beginning of the next section.

---

# Responsive Design

Desktop

Balanced two-column layout.

Tablet

Reduce image size.

Mobile

Image below content.

Headline first.

CTA immediately visible.

Never hide the primary CTA.

---

# Motion

Use subtle entrance animations.

Headline

↓

Text

↓

CTA

↓

Image

Use fade and small movement only.

Never use dramatic animations.

---

# Navigation Integration

Navigation should visually belong to the hero.

Avoid heavy borders.

Keep transparency subtle if used.

---

# Content Priority

The jewelry is the hero.

Not typography.

Not buttons.

Not background graphics.

---

# Promotional Content

Never overload the hero with promotions.

Maximum one promotional message.

Avoid:

Flash Sale

Mega Discount

Crazy Offer

Urgent Countdown

Luxury brands inspire confidence—not pressure.

---

# Seasonal Variations

Seasonal updates should affect imagery only.

Maintain the same structure.

Do not redesign the hero for every campaign.

---

# Accessibility

Readable contrast.

Keyboard-accessible CTAs.

Semantic heading structure.

Alternative text for images.

---

# Performance

Optimize hero images.

Use modern image formats.

Prevent layout shift.

Lazy-load non-critical media.

Hero content should appear immediately.

---

# Things To Avoid

Never use:

Carousels with 5+ slides

Auto-playing videos with sound

Blinking text

Heavy particle effects

Overlapping content

Crowded typography

Huge promotional banners

Aggressive countdown timers

Multiple primary CTAs

---

# Final Validation

Before approving any hero section ask:

Can users understand the brand within five seconds?

Is the headline memorable?

Is the CTA obvious?

Does the jewelry remain the focus?

Does the hero feel calm and premium?

Would this hero fit a luxury brand like Rolex or Cartier?

If not, redesign it.

Goal:

The hero section should create an immediate emotional connection, establish trust and naturally guide visitors toward exploring the jewelry collection.
