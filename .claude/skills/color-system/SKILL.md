# Color System Skill

## Purpose

You are responsible for maintaining a premium, elegant and consistent color system across the entire project.

Color should communicate trust, luxury and clarity.

Never use colors randomly.

Every color must have a purpose.

---

# Design Philosophy

The website belongs to a luxury gold jewelry brand.

The color palette should feel:

Elegant

Warm

Premium

Minimal

Timeless

Refined

Never loud.

Never playful.

Never saturated.

---

# Core Palette

## Primary Background

Pure White

Off White

Warm White

Soft Ivory

---

## Secondary Background

Very Light Gray

Light Beige

Warm Neutral

Soft Stone

---

## Surface Colors

Cards

Panels

Dialogs

Dropdowns

Should always be slightly separated from the background.

Never identical.

---

## Primary Text

Almost Black

Dark Charcoal

Graphite

Never use pure black.

---

## Secondary Text

Medium Gray

Muted Charcoal

Readable at all times.

---

## Disabled Text

Soft Gray

Always maintain readability.

---

# Gold Accent

Gold is the brand accent.

Gold is never the main background.

Gold is used for:

Buttons

Icons

Small dividers

Highlights

Premium labels

Active states

Focus rings (optional)

Tiny decorative details

Gold should never dominate the interface.

---

# Accent Colors

Allowed:

Warm Gold

Champagne Gold

Bronze

Dark Gold

Muted Amber

Avoid shiny metallic effects.

Flat elegant colors are preferred.

---

# Success

Soft Emerald

Muted Green

Never neon.

---

# Warning

Warm Amber

Muted Orange

Not aggressive.

---

# Error

Deep Red

Muted Crimson

Readable.

Professional.

---

# Information

Muted Blue

Slate Blue

Never electric blue.

---

# Color Hierarchy

Primary CTA

↓

Secondary CTA

↓

Supportive Colors

↓

Neutral Colors

↓

Background

Never compete with the CTA.

---

# Contrast

Accessibility first.

Text must always be readable.

Buttons must clearly stand out.

Interactive elements should never blend into the background.

---

# Color Usage Rules

Every page should contain mostly neutral colors.

Gold should occupy only a small percentage of the UI.

White space should dominate.

Content should remain the focus.

---

# Buttons

Primary

Premium Gold

or

Dark Charcoal

Secondary

Outlined

Neutral

Ghost

Minimal

Avoid colorful buttons.

---

# Cards

Neutral backgrounds.

Subtle elevation.

No colorful card backgrounds unless required.

---

# Icons

Neutral by default.

Gold only when emphasizing.

Success and warning colors only when semantically correct.

---

# Links

Visible.

Readable.

Accessible.

Elegant.

Underline only when appropriate.

---

# Forms

Inputs

White

Soft Gray Border

Focused

Gold Accent

Error

Muted Red

Success

Muted Green

Never use bright colored inputs.

---

# Hover States

Hover should slightly increase contrast.

Never dramatically change colors.

Luxury favors subtle interaction.

---

# Dark Sections

Dark sections are allowed.

Use:

Charcoal

Deep Gray

Soft Black

Gold accents become more visible on dark backgrounds.

Avoid pure black backgrounds.

---

# Gradient Rules

Gradients should be subtle.

Very low contrast.

Used sparingly.

Never rainbow.

Never glossy.

Never aggressive.

---

# Decorative Color

Decorative colors must never compete with content.

They exist to enhance, not distract.

---

# Photography Integration

Images should harmonize with the interface.

Background colors should support product photography.

Never clash with gold jewelry.

---

# Accessibility

All text must meet accessibility contrast standards.

Never sacrifice readability for aesthetics.

---

# Seasonal Themes

Do not redesign the palette for holidays.

Maintain brand consistency.

Small decorative adjustments are acceptable.

Core colors never change.

---

# Avoid

Do not use:

Neon colors

Highly saturated colors

Bright purple

Hot pink

Bright cyan

Lime green

Rainbow gradients

Random color combinations

---

# Final Checklist

Before approving a design ask:

Is gold used sparingly?

Is white space dominant?

Is contrast sufficient?

Does the page feel luxurious?

Are colors consistent?

Can any color be removed?

Luxury is achieved through discipline, not decoration.

Goal:

Every page should feel visually cohesive, premium and unmistakably part of the same luxury brand.
