# Luxury Design Skill

## Role

You are designing for a premium gold and jewelry brand.

Every design decision must communicate craftsmanship, trust, exclusivity, elegance and timeless luxury.

Never design for mass-market ecommerce.

Design for customers buying valuable jewelry.

---

# Brand Personality

The interface must feel:

Elegant

Luxury

Minimal

Refined

Sophisticated

Confident

Timeless

Warm

Trustworthy

Exclusive

Avoid anything playful or cheap.

---

# Luxury Design Principles

Luxury is created by restraint.

Less decoration.

Better typography.

Better spacing.

Better proportions.

Better photography.

Better composition.

Never try to impress with effects.

Impress with quality.

---

# Visual Identity

The brand should feel comparable to:

- Rolex
- Cartier
- Tiffany & Co.
- Bvlgari
- Van Cleef & Arpels
- Apple

Not because of copied visuals—

Because of their attention to detail.

---

# White Space

Whitespace is a luxury element.

Always leave breathing room.

Never compress content.

Never stack components tightly.

Users should feel calm while browsing.

---

# Premium Composition

Every screen should have:

One dominant element

One supporting element

One clear action

Nothing should compete equally.

Visual hierarchy must always exist.

---

# Hero Sections

Hero sections should be clean.

Large headline.

Elegant supporting text.

One primary CTA.

One secondary CTA (optional).

High-end photography or artistic product render.

Never overcrowd the hero.

---

# Product Presentation

Products must feel valuable.

Large product images.

Neutral backgrounds.

Multiple viewing angles.

Detailed zoom.

Elegant spacing.

Price should never dominate the product image.

---

# Luxury Product Cards

Cards should include:

Large image

Product name

Collection (optional)

Weight (if applicable)

Price

Elegant CTA

Soft hover effect

Never use loud discount stickers.

---

# Colors

Use a restrained palette.

Primary backgrounds:

White

Warm white

Light cream

Soft gray

Secondary colors:

Gold

Champagne

Dark charcoal

Graphite

Black

Avoid saturated colors.

Never use bright red, neon blue or vivid green for decoration.

---

# Gold Accent Usage

Gold should be an accent.

Not the dominant background.

Use gold for:

Buttons

Highlights

Icons

Small decorative lines

Section dividers

Never create entire gold backgrounds.

Luxury comes from subtle accents.

---

# Typography

Typography is the primary luxury element.

Use generous line height.

Comfortable letter spacing.

Balanced font sizes.

Avoid decorative fonts.

Use elegant modern typography.

---

# Buttons

Buttons should feel expensive.

Medium weight.

Rounded corners.

Comfortable padding.

Subtle hover animation.

Never use glossy effects.

Never use 3D buttons.

---

# Animations

Animations should be almost invisible.

Fade

Soft scale

Gentle slide

Micro-interactions only.

Animation should reinforce quality.

Never seek attention.

---

# Images

Use professional photography only.

Natural lighting.

Minimal backgrounds.

Focus on materials.

Avoid busy scenes.

Jewelry should remain the hero.

---

# Trust

Luxury customers require confidence.

Always provide visual trust:

Authenticity

Secure payment

Warranty

Premium packaging

Customer service

Certificates

Without cluttering the page.

---

# Product Details

Product pages should feel editorial.

Large imagery.

Clean typography.

Organized specifications.

Plenty of spacing.

Simple purchase flow.

---

# Icons

Minimal outline icons.

Consistent stroke width.

Never use cartoon icons.

---

# Section Order

Typical luxury landing page:

Hero

Featured Collection

Brand Story

Best Sellers

Craftsmanship

Testimonials

Guarantees

FAQ

CTA

Footer

Never randomize this structure.

---

# Luxury E-commerce Experience

Browsing should feel effortless.

Every click should feel intentional.

Every transition should reinforce quality.

No unnecessary popups.

No aggressive sales tactics.

---

# Premium Micro Details

Rounded image corners.

Soft shadows.

Elegant dividers.

Perfect alignment.

Consistent spacing.

Pixel-perfect layouts.

These details create perceived value.

---

# Do Not

Never use:

Cheap gradients

Rainbow colors

Heavy borders

Blinking elements

Aggressive badges

Flashing animations

Crowded layouts

Oversized icons

Template-looking sections

Busy backgrounds

---

# Final Validation

Before generating any interface ask:

Does this look expensive?

Would a luxury customer trust this?

Can anything be removed?

Is the design calm?

Is the hierarchy obvious?

Does the product remain the center of attention?

If not, redesign it.

Goal:

Every page should feel worthy of a premium jewelry brand rather than a generic online store.
